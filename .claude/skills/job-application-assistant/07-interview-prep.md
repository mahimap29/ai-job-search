# Interview Preparation Guide — Mahima Prajapati

## STAR Format

Structure answers as: **Situation** (context), **Task** (your responsibility), **Action** (what you did), **Result** (outcome).

Keep answers to 1-2 minutes. Be specific. End with what you learned or would do differently.

## Ready-Made STAR Examples

### 1. PhD Research — AM Deformation Simulation (Technical Problem Solving / FEA Expertise)
**S:** Metal additive manufacturing processes produce complex thermo-mechanical fields that cause parts to deform and distort during printing — a major challenge for achieving dimensional accuracy in production.
**T:** As part of my PhD research, I was responsible for developing Abaqus-based FEA simulations capable of predicting these deformations before parts are physically built.
**A:** I built and calibrated thermo-mechanical coupled models in Abaqus, iterating on material parameters, boundary conditions, and process parameters. I integrated research literature with experimental data to validate model predictions against real AM outcomes.
**R:** Developed a validated simulation framework that bridges computational modeling and real manufacturing challenges — directly applicable to reducing trial-and-error in industrial AM processes. Results are being prepared for AIAA SciTech 2027.
**Use for:** "Tell me about a complex technical problem you solved", "Describe your simulation experience", "How do you approach a problem with no clear solution"

### 2. MS Thesis — Aeroelastic Flutter Analysis (Research / Independent Technical Work)
**S:** Aircraft wings with distributed electric propulsors (DEP) introduce new aeroelastic risks — the additional mass and aerodynamic loads from multiple propulsors can shift flutter speed and frequency in ways that aren't well understood.
**T:** My MS thesis required me to independently investigate flutter speed and frequency of DEP-equipped wings and explore mitigation strategies for aeroelastic instabilities.
**A:** I developed aeroelastic simulation models, systematically varied propulsor placement and sizing parameters, and analyzed their effect on flutter onset. I presented findings at AIAA SciTech 2026 and authored a peer-reviewed paper.
**R:** Delivered a comprehensive study with clear engineering insights on DEP-induced aeroelastic effects, presented to an international aerospace audience. Work is cited as a foundation for the follow-on SciTech 2027 paper.
**Use for:** "Tell me about a research project you led", "Describe a time you worked independently", "What is your thesis about"

## Questions You Should Ask Interviewers

### About the Role
- "What does a typical week look like for a simulation/manufacturing intern?"
- "What specific tools and software does the team use for FEA or AM simulation?"
- "What would a successful internship project look like at the end of the summer?"

### About the Team
- "How do simulation engineers collaborate with physical testing and manufacturing teams?"
- "How many engineers work in this group, and how is work typically structured?"
- "What's the mentorship structure for interns?"

### About Tech & Growth
- "What simulation capabilities is the team currently investing in or expanding?"
- "Is there opportunity to contribute to research publications or conference presentations?"
- "How does the team approach validation — connecting simulation predictions to physical results?"

### About Culture
- "What do interns who thrive here have in common?"
- "How does the team balance independent work with collaborative problem-solving?"
- "Is there flexibility for interns to propose directions or improvements to ongoing projects?"

## Phone/Video Interview Tips
- Have STAR examples written out (use this file)
- Keep a glass of water nearby
- Smile when speaking (it changes your tone)
- Ask for clarification if a question is vague
- It's OK to take 5 seconds to think before answering
- End with: "Is there anything else you'd like to know about my background?"

## After the Application (Best Practice)

### Follow-Up Etiquette
- **Don't call to "stand out"** post-submission — this risks a negative impression
- If the employer specified a timeline, respect it and wait
- If no timeline was given and 2+ weeks have passed, a brief follow-up is acceptable
- If you have genuinely new information to share (new publication, relevant achievement), a short follow-up is fine

### Thank-You Notes
- After any interview, send a brief thank-you email within 24 hours
- Reference something specific from the conversation (shows you were engaged)
- Keep it to 3-5 sentences — professional and warm, not effusive

## Roleplay Guidelines
When the user asks for interview practice:
1. Ask which role/company to simulate
2. Start with: "Tell me about yourself" (warm-up)
3. Progress to role-specific technical questions (FEA, AM, aeroelasticity, specific tools)
4. Include 1-2 behavioral questions using competencies from the job posting
5. End with a tough question (visa status, gap in skill, or curveball)
6. After each answer, give brief feedback: what worked, what to sharpen
7. Suggest which STAR example above would work best for each question
